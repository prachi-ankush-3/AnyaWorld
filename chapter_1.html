Chapter 1

A Girl Who Could Hear Hearts

The beginning of Anya's unforgettable adventure.

Once upon a time, in a world where peace was fragile and countries secretly watched one another, there lived a little girl with a very unusual gift.

Her name was Anya.

She looked like any ordinary child—with bright green eyes, short pink hair, and a cheerful smile. But hidden behind that innocent face was a secret that no one else knew.

Anya could read people's minds.

She had not been born with this power. Long ago, scientists in a mysterious laboratory had performed experiments on her. One day, she escaped from that lonely place and wandered into an orphanage.

Life there was difficult.

Many families came to adopt children, but none kept Anya for very long. Whenever she accidentally reacted to thoughts that she should never have known, people believed she was strange or troublesome. Again and again, she was returned.

Even so, Anya never gave up hoping.

"I just want a family who will stay with me forever."

Every night before falling asleep, she wished for the same thing.

One bright morning, the orphanage door opened.

A tall man wearing a neat suit stepped inside. His expression was calm, and his sharp blue eyes carefully observed everything around him.

His name was Loid Forger.

At least, that was the name everyone knew.

In reality, he was the greatest spy in the country, known only by his secret codename...

Twilight.

His newest mission was unlike anything he had ever done before.

To prevent a war between two powerful nations, he had to create a fake family. He needed a wife... and most importantly, a child who could attend the prestigious Eden Academy.

As soon as Loid entered the room, Anya heard something surprising.

"I need an intelligent child for my mission."

Her eyes sparkled.

"Mission? He's a spy!"

She loved spy cartoons more than anything. To Anya, becoming part of a spy's family sounded like the greatest adventure imaginable.

Determined to be chosen, she eagerly answered every question before Loid even asked it. She solved a crossword puzzle that he was thinking about, making him wonder if she was much smarter than she appeared.

The orphanage director smiled proudly.

"She's a very bright child."

Loid nodded.

"I'll adopt her."

Those simple words filled Anya's heart with happiness.

For the first time in a long while, someone had chosen her.

Their new apartment wasn't large, but to Anya, it felt like a palace.

She wandered through every room with sparkling eyes, imagining secret spy headquarters hidden behind every wall.

Loid explained that he was a psychiatrist and that they needed to behave like a normal father and daughter.

Anya smiled quietly.

"I know you're actually a spy."

End of Chapter 1
